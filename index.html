About the Artist

CHERI HOFFMAN was born in Saint Louis, Missouri. Hoffman received a BA in Psychology from the University of Denver in 1978. She studied conceptual art and painting at Washington University, School of Art (now Sam Fox School of Design & Visual Arts) from 1994-98.

Hoffman’s home and studio have become an integral part of her creative process. The light, space, and architecture of these places are mirrored and inextricably linked to the art she creates. As well, she has an appreciation for and is a keen observer of nature, people, design, form, function, beauty. There is a distinct sensibility, a thread, rooted in the natural world and these appreciations are reflected in her work. The images almost always reflect a lifelong study of light, dimension and form. Using the alchemy of oil and wax along with pencil, paint and monotype, she explores the use of pattern, texture, surface, and light. Hoffman lives and works in Saint Louis, Missouri.

Hoffman’s work is process driven--experimentation chance/accident is how most interesting marks are discovered and logged. She has a passion for natural materials, their properties, texture, age, translucency, ability to conduct light or ability to hold a delicate or dense mark. These materials include many types of paper, oil paint, charcoal, graphite and wax. Often it is the synergy of certain materials with each other that create the rich outcome.

Each body of work is planned only in that she invests heavily in time and labor in preparation of materials and creating a ground-to transform paper into receptive fields/grounds for mark-marking, whether for bold strong gestures or more quiet vulnerable marks. Those initial marks and gestures are quick and intuitive, without any sketches or drawings in advance.That then triggers a creative response. For example the compositional pieces such as the rorschach with a bird or flower Everything else unfolds. There is never an outcome in mind which, when successful, is (?). She chooses the materials from past experimentation and builds on that. With each successive project comes new discoveries giving the work energy. When working this way, the marks are made quickly

Cheri’s goal is to distill and share the images that convey or elicit universal emotional states that most everyone can relate to at one time or another–the common human experience. Heaviness, levity, ecstasy
